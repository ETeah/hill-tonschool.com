Meet the Founder of Hill-ton School

I am Newton N. Gaye, also known by my people as Rufu N. Weyeah, and I was born and raised in Liberia. Though I was fortunate enough to attend a nearby school, the amount of resources for education in my environment was extremely small. As a student, sharing one book with the other children, made it difficult to learn at my own pace or focus. I eventually learned how to read and write, but at very low level. I am sure I would have learn much faster and better if I had a book of my own. I still remember thinking about how life would be if my country was as rich as the United State. I used to day dream of going to the library, sitting behind computers and doing my works as fast as I could. My dream came through during my tenth grade year in 1992, when I, along with a few young men were sent off to the United States of America. I was so exited! I was given this opportunity to get better education, and to go back home and help my country.

My Education

I attended the University City High School in Philadelphia, in the United States. I was placed in the ESL because I did not meet the reading standard of an American tenth grader. However, I was determined to prove my capabilities. So, I spent most of my time reading and studying at home, and at the school libraries. In the fall of 1995, I was accepted into a baccalaureate program at West Chester University of Pennsylvania. In the spring of 2000, I graduated from DPT Business School for Computer Services in Northeast Philadelphia. I married Edna Duo, a woman from Liberia. We had six children together. All of my children attended schools in the Philadelphia area. So far, one has graduated from Gannon University in Pennsylvania. I am thankful for the wonderful life my children get to have in this country because of the opportunities that are available here.

Making Dreams Come Through For Other People

It is my goal to provide the children of Liberia with equal opportunities for education and the potential for a better life. Edna and I decided to build a fifteen room school in Paynesville, Liberia, where there are no local schools for the children to attend. With the help of my sister-in-law, Debbie Duo, an Accountant with a degree from University of Liberia, construction began and is already well under-way! The new learning community, HILL-TON SCHOOL OF THE FUTURE, named after our two youngest children, Hillary and Newton II, will host Kindergarten through High School level students. Our plans include an auditorium, library and computer lab; which, we will open to University students, as well as our own students, who wish to study within our facility. We have appointed a school Principal, University of Liberia student, Leamon Duo, who eagerly awaits her student body made up of over 500 children. Each room is 40’x40’, with 15’ high ceilings and large windows to allow for ample light and air ventilation to keep the children comfortable while learning. Edna and I purchased a 1.5 acre plot of land beside the site of the main school; where we hope to one day expand the campus, adding on a science lab. My wife and I have invested everything we have into this project and we won’t quit until we reach our goal of providing equal educational opportunities to the children of Liberia, but we need your help! We are seeking assistance from anyone passionate about children, education, reading, technology, and bettering the lives of deserving people.


Liberia is one of the oldest independent countr in Africa. We need your help to make our nation the best it can be. We need eduction.
